# Jobs for Novaks

I am, $deity willing, going to graduate from grad school soon, with a Ph.D. in bioinformatics. I am looking for jobs in this field.

Here is a list of companies I might want to work at. Some of these won't work geographically with where I want to live, but they're the *sorts* of companies I want to work at. If you work at companies like this, you should definitely hire me.

* [Desktop Genetics](https://www.deskgen.com/landing/company#about-careers) is building a fancy CRISPR CAD system, or, as they put it, "building an AI to re-engineer the human genome".
* [Caribou Biosciences](http://cariboubio.com/careers) apparently owns CRISPR?
* [GenePeeks](https://www.genepeeks.com/careers/) takes genomic data from two people and tries to predict the risk that their child might have of being conceived with any of a number of genetic diseases.
* [Ginkgo Bioworks](http://www.ginkgobioworks.com/careers/) engineers better organisms for biosynthesis.
* [Dovetail Genomics](https://dovetailgenomics.com/company/careers/) has a cool distantly-paired-read technology that presents interesting bioinformatics challenges.
* [Helix](https://www.helix.com/careers/) is hiring lots of people to do some kind of personal genomics thing.
* [Twist](https://www.twistbioscience.com/careers/) makes oligos on silicon somehow.
* I know a guy at [DNANexus](https://www.dnanexus.com/careers) where they do data analysis on cloud infrastructure.
* I know a lady at [Agilent](http://careers.agilent.com/) where they make microarrays.
* These startup people have a [whole list of companies](https://angel.co/bioinformatics).
* I could alspo work for some kind of as-yet-nonexistent biohacker cooperative.
